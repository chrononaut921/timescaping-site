01 — The Problem

MOST TEAMS THINK ABOUT
THE FUTURE REGULARLY.
ALL OF IT HAPPENS FROM
INSIDE THE PRESENT.

Strategy sessions, planning days, quarterly reviews. The same assumptions, the same reference points, the same people thinking in the same ways. The future gets discussed. It rarely gets felt.

Timescaping changes the conditions under which that conversation happens.

02 — What Happens

THE 100-YEAR
DEVICE

Your team works with scenario cards set 100 years from now. Not to predict anything. The distance is a device. Far enough that nobody has the right answer. Far enough that expertise stops being a shield.

People stop performing. They start thinking. Conversations happen that don't normally happen at work. Assumptions surface. Disagreements become curious rather than defensive.

The session doesn't produce a strategy. It changes the atmosphere in which strategy later happens.
