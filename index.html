01 — Why it matters

THE WORLD IS CHANGING FASTER THAN WE CAN MAKE SENSE OF IT TOGETHER.

That is not a metaphor. The systems we live inside are transforming at a pace that outstrips our collective ability to process what is happening, let alone imagine what comes next.

This creates a specific kind of disorientation. Not lack of information. Not lack of intelligence. A lag. Between how fast things move and how slowly humans integrate change emotionally, ethically, imaginatively.

People reach for familiar responses. They extrapolate the present. They collapse complexity into doom or inevitability. Not because they are passive. Because they have never been given space to do anything else.

Timescaping is one attempt to create that space.

02 — What Timescaping is

THE
100-YEAR
DEVICE

Groups step 100 years into the future together. Using scenario cards built around specific themes, from android rights to climate restoration to the meaning of work, participants read to each other, react, and follow the conversation wherever it goes.

Far enough that nobody has the right answer. Far enough that expertise stops being a shield. Far enough that stories about what is inevitable start to loosen.

The future is the mirror. The present is what we are actually examining.

03 — What happens when people imagine together

SIT TWO PEOPLE
WITH THE SAME SCENARIO.
ONE FINDS IT LIBERATING.
THE OTHER FINDS IT
DISTURBING.

Neither can fully explain why. That gap is where the interesting thing happens.

People discover assumptions they didn't know they were making.

They find unexpected common ground with someone they thought they disagreed with.

They encounter their own values before those values have hardened into opinions.

No expertise advantage. No right answer. No position to defend.

That quality of conversation is rarer than it sounds. It is also the thing people remember longest.

04 — What happens in a session

A SESSION MOVES THROUGH LEVELS.

I

CALIBRATION

Simple cards in small groups. People get used to talking without agenda, without needing to perform.

II

DEPTH

Pairs work with more complex scenarios, reading to each other, reacting, following wherever it goes.

III

RETURN

A final reflection brings the group back together. What surfaced reconnects to the world they actually live in.
